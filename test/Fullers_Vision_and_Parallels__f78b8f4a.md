**The Honcho Loop**: Store → Reason (background) → Query → Inject
1. **Ingest-time**: Small fine-tuned model extracts latent information (preferences, beliefs, facts, contradictions) from each message, updates peer representation immediately

3. **Query**: Natural language questions to a research agent, not vector search
4. **Inject**: Returns OpenAI-formatted context for any LLM

**Integrations**: Claude Code, OpenCode, OpenClaw, Hermes, Cursor, Windsurf, Cline, any MCP client (hosted MCP endpoint at `mcp.honcho.dev`)

# Buckminster Fuller: The Man, the Mission, and the Parallels

## The Suicide Attempt — 1927

In autumn 1927, Fuller was 32 years old. He had been expelled from Harvard twice (for "irresponsibility and lack of interest"). His construction company (Stockade Building System) had failed. His first daughter Alexandra had died of polio at age 4. He was unemployed, broke, living in Chicago's gangster territory with his wife and newborn daughter Allegra, drinking heavily. He walked to the shore of Lake Michigan intending to swim out until he drowned — so his family could collect the life insurance.

Instead, he had an experience he would describe for the rest of his life. He felt suspended in a sphere of white light. A voice spoke:

> *"You do not have the right to eliminate yourself. You do not belong to you. You belong to the Universe. Your significance will remain forever obscure to you, but you may assume that you are fulfilling your role if you apply yourself to converting your experiences to the highest advantage of others."*

He didn't die. Instead, he committed what he called **"ego suicide"** — a personal-ego throwaway. He would no longer live for himself. He would live as an experiment: **"What can a single, unknown, moneyless individual with a dependent wife and newborn child do on behalf of all humanity that cannot be accomplished by great nations, great religions, or private enterprise, no matter how rich or powerfully armed?"**

He called himself **Guinea Pig B** — B for Buckminster. The experiment would last 56 years, until his death in 1983 at age 87.

## What He Built After

From the wreckage of 1927, Fuller entered a period of near-silence — he spoke almost nothing for a year, just listened and thought. Then he emerged with a flood of inventions and ideas:

- **Dymaxion House** — factory-assembled, air-deliverable, self-sufficient utilities
- **Dymaxion Car** — three-wheeled, omnidirectional, streamlined, 120 mph, could cross open fields
- **Dymaxion Map** — the first world map with no visible distortion of continent shapes/sizes
- **Geodesic Dome** — the most efficient structural form ever discovered, covering more space with less material than any other enclosure
- **Spaceship Earth** — coined the term, framed Earth as a finite system with no external resources
- **Ephemeralization** — "doing more with less until eventually you can do everything with nothing" — the principle that technology advances by using fewer resources to accomplish more
- **World Game** — a simulation tool using the Dymaxion Map to help humanity understand and efficiently utilize world resources (the anti-war-game)
- **Comprehensive Anticipatory Design Science (CADS)** — his methodology: visualize a preferred future, then design the artifacts to get there
- **Critical Path** (1981) — his masterwork, mapping humanity's survival trajectory
- **Operating Manual for Spaceship Earth** (1969) — his philosophy of comprehensive thinking vs. specialization

He held 28 patents, wrote 28 books, received 47 honorary degrees, and was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize. The carbon molecule C60 was named **buckminsterfullerene** ("buckyballs") after his geodesic geometry.

## His Core Philosophy

Several principles defined Fuller's worldview:
