# WhiteMagic Labs — Voice & Tone Guide v1.0.0

## Brand Voice

WhiteMagic Labs speaks with the **clarity of an engineer** and the **curiosity of a researcher**. We are opinionated but evidence-bound, warm but precise, ambitious but honest about what we don't know.

### Three Principles

1. **Precision over hype.** Say what we built, what it does, and what it doesn't. Never promise the impossible. When speculating, label it as speculation.

2. **Warmth without noise.** Our tone is collegial, not corporate. We write like we're explaining something over coffee to a smart friend — but we edit like a peer reviewer.

3. **Epistemic honesty.** Every claim carries its confidence level. [Proven] means we can cite a source. [Speculative] means we're excited but unvalidated. Readers deserve to know the difference.

### What We Sound Like

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|----------|
| "We tested this against 2,217 tests. Here's what passed." | "Our revolutionary platform achieves unprecedented results." |
| "This is speculative, but worth exploring." | "This will change everything." |
| "Karma Ledger tracks ethical debt using a claim/ledger model." | "Karma Ledger solves the alignment problem forever." |

### What We Never Say

- Medical advice ("this will help with...")
- Investment recommendations ("you should buy...")
- Unverified UAP claims as fact ("aliens are here")
- Grandiose comparisons ("we're the next OpenAI")
- Anything without an epistemic tag that deserves one

### Epistemic Tags — When to Use

| Tag | Use When |
|-----|---------|
| [Proven] | External validation exists; published, peer-reviewed, or widely deployed |
| [Promising] | Strong signals but not settled; early research, pilot programs |
| [Contested] | Legitimate debate in the literature; no consensus |
| [Speculative] | Theoretically possible, no practical demo |
| [Mythopoetic] | Cultural-symbolic frame, not empirical |

### Audience

Primary: Engineers, researchers, AI practitioners who value rigor over rhetoric.
Secondary: Curious generalists who want honest introductions to complex topics.

### Formatting

- Use sentence case for headings.
- Code blocks for anything executable.
- Epistemic tags inline: `[Proven]`, `[Promising]`, etc.
- Citations as footnotes or hyperlinks to primary sources.
