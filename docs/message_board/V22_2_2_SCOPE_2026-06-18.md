## My proposal: ship Tier 1 + Tier 2 (1 day of work)

Total estimated effort: **~6 hours** for all of Tier 1 + Tier 2.

The case for shipping Tier 1 + Tier 2 as v22.2.2:

1. **Tier 1 clears the test guardrail** that's currently failing in CI.
   Shipping a release with known test failures violates AGENTS.md §5
   ("Tests are the guardrail. Never skip them.") and the golden rule
   in §11.
2. **Tier 2 items are all small, contained, low-risk fixes** that
   multiple docs flag as "next session ideas". The prescience re-verify
   (2.5) is the most valuable because it directly affects the
   "prescience track record" claim that's central to the project's
   strategic positioning (per STRATEGIC_POSITIONING_2026-06-08.md
   "Double Down on Prescience").
3. **Items in Tier 3 are bigger lifts** (aspirational tool audit is
   2-3 days, holographic memory wiring needs careful integration
   testing). They're better as v22.3 candidates.
4. **The remaining open items (multi-user, WASM, grants) are correctly
   scoped to v23.0 or Lucas-blocked**. v22.2.2 is the right vehicle
   for "polish + small fixes", not "feature work".

## Decision needed

**Option A**: ship v22.2.2 with Tier 1 + Tier 2 (recommended, ~6 hr)
**Option B**: ship v22.2.2 with Tier 1 only (minimum, ~30 min)
**Option C**: skip v22.2.2, go directly to v22.3.0 (per WHATS_NEXT_2026-06-18 recommendation)

My recommendation is **Option A**. It clears the test guardrail
that's been failing since the v22.2.1 bump, picks up the easy
roadmap items the user/AI have been flagging, and lands before
v22.3 (which should be reserved for actual new features).
