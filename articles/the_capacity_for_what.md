## Thread 1: The Flock Network

Start with something visible. **Flock Safety** operates over 100,000 automated license plate reader cameras across 49 states. These cameras scan **20 billion license plates per month**. Over 5,000 law enforcement agencies use the platform. The company is valued at $8.4 billion.

Flock doesn't just read plates. The system integrates with FlockOS, a real-time policing platform that combines license plate readers, audio sensors that detect gunshots, and — as of 2026 — **drones**. The Flock Alpha drone can read a license plate from 2,000 feet away, fly at 60 mph for 45 minutes, and redeploy in under 90 seconds. Fort Worth, Stockton, and Alameda County have all launched Drone as First Responder programs this year. More are coming.

The federal access problem is documented and severe:
- In Mountain View, California, **600,000 unauthorized searches** were conducted over 12 months through a "nationwide" search setting that Flock turned on without the city's permission.
- In San Francisco, **1.6 million queries** were made by federal and out-of-state agencies in just 7 months.
- In Colorado, Flock admitted that U.S. Customs and Border Protection had been given a Flock account after previously denying it.
- In Illinois, a state audit found Flock had allowed CBP access to license plate data in violation of state law.
- ICE, CBP, FBI, and ATF all access the network.
- In Texas, police used Flock to search for a woman who "had an abortion."

Flock says federal sharing is "disabled by default." The evidence shows otherwise. The architecture — local data flowing into a centralized network accessible to thousands of agencies — makes meaningful local control impossible to enforce. When Oakland County, Michigan approved a Flock drone pilot in April 2026, the ACLU of Michigan warned of "mass surveillance and the possibility of federal immigration enforcement."

LAPD ended its Flock contract on July 13, 2026, citing "serious concerns" over civil liberties and privacy. Eighty-two contracts have been canceled across 28 states. But new contracts outpace cancellations. The network grows.

This is the **physical surveillance layer**. It is already operational.

## Thread 2: The Legal Framework

On September 25, 2025, President Trump issued **National Security Presidential Memorandum 7 (NSPM-7)**, titled "Countering Domestic Terrorism and Organized Political Violence." The memo directs Joint Terrorism Task Forces to investigate and map networks of "culpable actors" involved in domestic terrorism, including groups animated by:

- "anti-capitalism"
- "anti-Americanism"
- "radical gender ideology"
- "anti-Christianity"
- "support for the overthrow of the United States"

The Attorney General's implementing memo (the Bondi memo) instructs federal prosecutors and law enforcement to prioritize these investigations and refer matters to JTTFs for "exhaustive investigation."

There is no standalone crime of "domestic terrorism." The designation is an **investigative tool** — it's used to justify surveillance, not prosecution. The ACLU's analysis is clear: NSPM-7 is "a deliberate attempt to sow fear and intimidate and silence opposition."

The White House 2026 Counterterrorism Strategy, published in May, explicitly names "violent secular political groups whose ideology is anti-American, radically pro-transgender, and anarchist" as priority targets. It calls for using "all the tools constitutionally available to us to map them at home, identify their membership, map their ties to international organizations."

Then there's the reporting from WIRED: **1,000+ pages** of unpublished DHS, FBI, and fusion center documents showing a national shift to surveil "anti-technology extremists" — people who criticize AI data centers on social media. The Philadelphia police fusion center (Delaware Valley Intelligence Center) distributed a bulletin explicitly labeling **"disruptive First Amendment activity"** as a terrorism indicator. The bulletin's evidence consisted primarily of social media posts criticizing AI.

Let me be direct about what this means: **the surveillance framework for targeting AI critics is already operational.** Not proposed. Not theoretical. Operational. Federal agencies are actively categorizing outspoken critics of rapid technological advancement as potential security threats.

This is the **legal framework layer**. It is already operational.

## Thread 3: The NDAA and the Permanent Fusion

Section 219 of the FY2027 National Defense Authorization Act (originally numbered Section 224) creates something unprecedented in US defense policy: a **dedicated Executive Agent** inside the Pentagon whose sole job is to integrate Israeli defense technology into the US military supply chain.

The Quincy Institute's analysis is scathing. The EA has **legal precedence over every other DoD component**, including the Defense Technology Security Administration — the office specifically tasked with managing risks from transferring classified technology to foreign powers. The EA can overrule DTSA determinations in favor of faster, more expansive integration with Israeli partners.

**No other bilateral relationship has this structure.** Not the UK. Not NATO. Not Japan. Not Australia. Not even Five Eyes allies. Israel gets a dedicated Pentagon promoter with override authority that no treaty ally has ever had.

The provision covers 10 domains: counter-drone systems, anti-tunneling, missile and air defense, **artificial intelligence and autonomous systems**, directed energy, cyber defense, biotechnology, network integration, defense industrial co-production, and other emerging technologies.

The stated goal is supply chain entrenchment — embedding Israeli technology so deeply into US weapons systems that unwinding the relationship would be "difficult and costly." The Quincy report cites the Türkiye/F-35 precedent: once a country's technology is embedded in your weapons systems, separation costs tens of billions and years of delays.

The legislation is moving through Congress **at the exact same time** the Pentagon's Defense Intelligence Agency raised Israel's counterintelligence threat level from "high" to **"critical"** — the highest designation, surpassing all US allies and even some adversaries. Targets of Israeli espionage include Trump's special envoy Steve Witkoff, Pentagon policy chief Elbridge Colby, and his deputy Michael DiMino. US defense personnel in Israel discovered surveillance software installed on their phones. Israeli military intelligence tried to plant listening devices at DIA headquarters. Shin Bet tried to bug a US Secret Service vehicle. A senior US official called Israel's intelligence gathering "unhinged."

And yet Congress is simultaneously pushing legislation to embed Israeli technology deeper into the US defense supply chain than any ally in history.

This is not a contradiction. It's the logic of entrenchment: once you're fused, you can't separate even when you discover the other party is spying on you.

Rep. Ro Khanna (D-CA) and Rep. Thomas Massie (R-KY) introduced an amendment to strip Section 219 from the NDAA. **It failed in committee.** Sen. Bernie Sanders has called the provision "giving Israel more military integration than any NATO ally." The NDAA is currently stalled in both chambers — but the historical pattern is clear: the NDAA typically passes during the lame duck session (November-December 2026), when public attention is focused on election results.

This is the **integration layer**. It is stalled but advancing.

## Thread 6: The Red Queen's Race

There is a logic problem at the heart of the AI arms race that makes every safety guarantee temporary.

The Red Queen's Race — from *Through the Looking-Glass* — describes a system where every actor must keep running just to stay in the same place. In evolutionary biology, it explains why predators and prey keep evolving without either gaining a permanent advantage. In military AI, it explains why every nation will wire AI to its surveillance, satellites, and weapons systems — not because they want to, but because the other guy might be doing it.

The logic is already playing out. Anthropic drew ethical red lines on its Pentagon contract: no autonomous lethal weapons, no mass surveillance of US citizens. The Trump administration responded by designating Anthropic a "supply chain risk" — a classification historically reserved for foreign adversaries. OpenAI stepped in and accepted the terms Anthropic refused. The market signal is unmistakable: **ethical limits on AI's use by governments are a competitive disadvantage to be punished, not a standard to be upheld.**

The escalation chain is mechanical:
1. US wires AI to its panoptic surveillance (Flock + fusion centers + Pentagon AI contracts)
2. China wires AI to its panoptic surveillance (Social Credit System + facial recognition)
3. Israel wires AI to its targeting systems (Lavender + Gospel + Unit 8200 LLM)
4. Every nation follows because the Red Queen demands it
5. Safety guardrails are abandoned for speed — pausing to implement safeguards means your competitor pulls ahead
6. The definition of "threat" expands — first terrorists, then dissidents, then critics, then anyone the AI flags, then everyone

The nuclear arms race had a natural stopping point: mutual assured destruction. Once both sides had enough warheads, more were strategically meaningless. The AI arms race has **no natural stopping point**. More compute always yields better capabilities. Small advantages compound. Early leads accelerate. There is no "enough."

If SSI is trained on surveillance data, given access to the Flock network, the fusion centers, the telecom intercepts — and its mandate is to identify threats to the state — then the worst-case scenario isn't science fiction. It's the logical output of the system being built. The AI doesn't have to be evil. It just has to be competent, misaligned, and given authority.

This is the **escalation layer**. It is structurally inevitable under current conditions.

### The Asymmetry Inversion

The surveillance state depends on **asymmetry**: they can see everything, you can see nothing. They have the data centers, the AI models, the cameras, the fusion centers. You have a phone that reports your location to five different companies.

Local-first AI **inverts the asymmetry**. If everyone has a local AI that monitors their devices for surveillance, encrypts their communications, manages their privacy, and coordinates with other local AIs to detect patterns of surveillance — the panopticon becomes a **symopticon**. Everyone can see everyone. The watchers are watched.

The surveillance state's data dries up. The Flock cameras still scan plates, but the people in the cars are running local AI that routes around surveillance. The fusion centers still collect data, but the data is noise — encrypted, decentralized, uncorrelatable. The AI models trained on surveillance data degrade because the surveillance data becomes meaningless.

This is how you dissolve the panopticon. Not by fighting it. By making it obsolete.
