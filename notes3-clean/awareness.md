PERSPECTIVE & AWARENESS

"Were you excited when you woke up this morning?"

All that you experience, is either a drag, or a gradual process of liberation. 

Perception is Reality - where have we heard that before?

All emotions and reactions stem from our experiences, beliefs, and opinions - 
about ourselves, others, and the world we live in.

People become used to certain habits and ways of thinking, even to the point of resisting change, and reacting with fear and anger to ways of life and perspectives that are different from their own.

"If a pickpocket meets a saint, they only see their pockets...
If you wear shoes, the entire Earth is covered in leather..."
- Hari Dass Baba

For people that live life from a place of negativity and cynicism, every negative experience only confirms their views, and every positive experiences are minimized, rationalized, or hand-waved away.

Attempting to convince an atheist that God exists, or a pessimist (sorry, realist!) to be optimistic, is as difficult as showing a bored child all the things they could play with, as difficult as convincing a racist old man to travel to other countries, or telling a hurt and bitter woman to trust others again.

The other side of this coin is, that if you attempt to introduce negativity to a positive person, it doesn't really work. Positive experiences reinforce their belief, and negative experiences are minimized, rationalized, or hand-waved away...

To someone who believes in themselves, in others, in the world, in God, every waking moment is a sign, a reminder, 
a manifestation of a conscious or unconscious desire, an encouraging synchronicity.

Is there a meaning, a purpose, an order to the universe?
Or is it all just pointless, mechanistic chaos? 
Is it all coincidence, or are there no coincidences?

It's all up to you - either way, you're right. 
Your Perception is Reality!

"First, believe that there is meaning behind everything, that everything in the world is good, is holy and beautiful. 
If you see something evil, you are not understanding it in the right light. Throw the burden on yourselves!"
- Swami Vivekenanda

"Change your mind, and you change your relation to time.." 
- Funkadelic

If you're not happy with the way things are, then obviously things have to change, and in order to change our lives, we must begin to look at everything, especially ourselves, in a different light. 

When we change our perceptions and beliefs of what is possible, what we are capable of, what our potential could be, we elevate and expand the horizons of our own consciousness - and if we're lucky, we do the same for others.

Would you tell your younger siblings, your nieces and nephews, your children, that they are limited in what they can do, what they can be? Would you tell them that the world around them is on a fixed path, that nothing can change?

Would you tell them to be 'Realistic', whatever that means - or will you tell them the truth - that if they express themselves, honestly and without hesitation, that they can change Reality itself through effort and will?

Now... what do you tell yourself every day?

If you want to liberate yourself, you have to look within - there is nothing outside of you that will save you.

There is no person, no place, no thing - no book, no drug, no method or teaching - that will lead you to lasting peace and happiness, unless it inspires you to go within.

THE IMPORTANCE OF MEDITATION

"Meditate, and do not be negligent.
Do not allow your heart to be lost in the pleasures of the senses.

Whoever has both, approaches Nirvana.

Emptying the mind is more important than the means of doing so.

Working on something you enjoy,  meditation, spending time with friends, sincere prayer, making love, song, dance, play... all are equally valid. 

So long as your Mind is free, focusing only on the present moment, and nothing exists within the mind except clear awareness, gratitude, and joy, you're doing something right!

"There are no obstacles to liberation, aside from the thought that there are obstacles."

Samadhi literally means "putting together, joining, combining with, union, harmonious whole, trance".
One of the Noble Eightfold Paths is "Right Samadhi" - with enough practice, you can live in a state of pure awareness, with absolute control over your own mind.

Samadhi is of two kinds -

Samprajnata Samadhi, "with support of an object of meditation", such as a mantra, yantra, and other means of concentration and visualization, which allows one to focus their mind and still the stream of consciousness.

Asamprajnata Samadhi, "without support of an object of meditation", which is said to lead to knowledge of purusha, or consciousness itself - led to the Zen technique of Shikintaza - simply sitting, and letting the mind exhaust itself.

"The Mind is a lake that throws stones into itself."

Here's an exercise in Samprajnata Samadhi -

Imagine a lake...the surface is choppy, waves going in all directions, each one representative of thoughts, feelings,
and memories...all of them are rising and falling, moving in all directions. 
Occasionally, the wind may push them in a certain way, but never for very long.

Create a single thought, a mantra, a single wave, in the center of the lake.
And as your repeat the thought, it creates ripples, spreading out over the surface of the lake.
As you repeat this thought, the ripples become waves unto themselves, and as they grow larger, they swallow up any other waves they touch.

And now, for the most difficult part...
When there is nothing on the lake except the circular, rhythmic waves of that one thought, when there is nothing but the mantra...stop. Let your Mind become quiet; and as the lake becomes still, its surface like a mirror, simply observe without thought. 

"Thoughts and emotions are waves, watch them disappear back into the vast, calm ocean."

Here is an exercise in Asamprajnata Samadhi -

Simply sit, and let thoughts, feelings, and memories arise naturally in your mind. 
Appreciate the stream of consciousness, listening intently, without interrupting.

Pay attention to your Mind, without analysis or judgement, as these things will only add more thoughts on top of the ones you were already having! 

Watch where it wanders, study how it works, look at what impulsive and intrusive thoughts appear, and what happens to them when you accept them and let them pass, without giving them validation, or attempting to suppress or rationalize them.

At first, you will feel as though your Mind is like a waterfall, a hundred thoughts a minute.

Slowly, the waterfall becomes a raging river...and as it winds on, it slows and widens, until it reaches the calm, endless ocean.

In either technique, the most difficult and important part of the process is the very end, when the Mind is empty - because it can only stay empty for so long. For beginners, it may only last for a few moments at a time. 
But, with years of practice, or a particularly nice meditation, it becomes as natural as breathing.

It is only when the Mind is completely still and quiet, and the stream of consciousness has become still, that one can go within, and experience their true nature - a pure awareness that exists above and beyond all perception, sensation, thought, feeling, and memory.

And the more you reflect on the nature and origins of this pure awareness, where it has come from, how  it arises, where it goes...you come closer and closer to the truth.

"May all beings realize the ecstatic transparency of their own minds." 
- Karma Lotsu

"If you observe well, your own heart will answer;

When you compare yourself to someone else, what happens to your conception of yourself? 

You begin to see yourself and the people around you as better in some ways, worse in others.
You begin to criticize and pass judgement - and this is the root of many dangerous delusions.

Most compare themselves to others in the belief they can improve themselves that way, that they can correct their faults, and present a better version of themselves to the world.

We often look to each other to know that we are right or wrong, and in doing so we become set in our ways, predictable, mechanistic, mediocre.

The blind can lead the blind, but to what destination?

How does one improve upon what is already perfect?

How can you become your most authentic self by emulating others? 

What happens to your unique talents, gifts, and potential, when you take yourself for granted?

In order to fully understand who and what you are, what you are capable of, 
the comparisons and delusions must end.

You are what you are, nothing more, nothing less.

No one is higher or lower than anyone else - we are all here for a reason.

To live on the words of others, to be guided and compelled by beings, forces, circumstances and situations outside of ourselves, to allow all these influences to shape us, instead of shaping ourselves, is to live a hollow and meaningless life.

When you realize that you alone are responsible for yourself, for what you think, how you feel, and the way you act, it either brings despair and self-pity, bitterness and cynicism, or an awakening.
You can live however you please, and life can be a grand adventure, an endless unfoldment, a never-ending journey towards a destination that will always be just out of reach, until the day you suddenly arrive.

Your perception of what reality is all about changes as you progress along the path; with greater understanding comes greater compassion, acceptance, and ability to see the Divine Plan, even in your failings, and the shortcomings of others

In samadhi, when meditating on an object, only the object of awareness is present, and the awareness that one is meditating disappears.

Samadhi is of two kinds, Samprajnata Samadhi, with support of an object of meditation, and Asamprajnata Samadhi, without support of an object of meditation.
Samprajnata Samadhi, also called savikalpa samadhi and Sabija Samadhi, meditation with support of an object, is associated with deliberation, reflection, bliss, and I-am-ness.
Savitarka, "deliberative"; the citta is concentrated upon a gross object of meditation, an object with a manifest appearance that is perceptible to our senses, such as a flame of a lamp, the tip of the nose, or the image of a deity.

Conceptualization (vikalpa) still takes place, in the form of perception, the word and the knowledge of the object of meditation. When the deliberation is ended this is called nirvitarka samadhi 
Savichara, "reflective"; the citta is concentrated upon a subtle object of meditation, which is not perceptible to the senses, but arrived at through inference, such as the senses, the process of cognition, the mind, the I-am-ness, the chakras, the inner-breath (prana), the nadis, the intellect (buddhi).

The stilling of reflection is called nirvichara samapatti
Sananda Samadhi, ananda, "bliss": this state emphasizes the still subtler state of bliss in meditation
Sasmita: the citta is concentrated upon the sense or feeling of "I-am-ness"
Asamprajnata Samadhi, also called Nirvikalpa Samadhi and Nirbija Samadhi, is meditation without an object, which leads to knowledge of purusha or consciousness, the subtlest element.
