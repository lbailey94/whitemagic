# Harmony

HARMONY 


Ë´ß


Harmony, balance, and temperance, in all things.

Learn how to face all things with grace, balancing inner and external energies.

When one loses their balance, they begin to react to external circumstances, instead of acting from internal direction.

Strive for harmony between the laws of nature, and the laws of man.

When people no longer revere peace and harmony, they covet power and influence, fame and fortune, to restore that which they feel they lost, to claim that which they have never had.

When people live in balance, they live comfortably, in contentment and peace, relying on and trusting in one another.

We have all the resources and technology we need, what we lack is compassion.

We must create a new civilization, centered around creating a better future, the restoration and protection of nature, rights and freedoms, and the cultivation of peace and understanding.

Our collective problems are a result of our collective action, and inaction. In order to free ourselves, we must all decide, of our own free will, to live in lasting peace and harmony.

"If someone finds a wise person, who can skillfully point out shortcomings and give suitable proof, cherish that person as a revealer of treasure. Only good can come from knowing them.

Let the wise guide, correct, and deter others from what is base and vile, he will be treasured by the good, and spurned by evil.

Do not choose bad friends. Do not choose people of low habits.
Select good friends. Be discriminating. Choose what is best.

Whoever drinks deeply of Dharma lives happily, with a peaceful mind."

"Truly, we dwell in happiness:
Surrounded by those that hate, we do not;
Surrounded by those that are sick, we are not;
Surrounded by those who struggle, we are free;
Having no obstacles at all, we feed upon joy, like radiant gods.

Victory creates hostility, 
For the defeated live in grief and shame;
Releasing both victory and defeat,
All are tranquil, and live in happiness.

There is no fire like lust, no evil like hatred;
There are no disasters equal to the attachments to Samsara;
There is no happiness greater than the peace of Nirvana.

Hunger is the worst disease,
Conditioned things are the worst calamity.
When we see this clearly, we attain nirvana, ultimate bliss.

Health is the greatest prize.
Contentment is the greatest wealth.
A loyal friend is the best relative.
Nirvana is the supreme bliss.

Having tasted of solitude and tranquility,
One becomes free of sorrow and sin,
Savoring the nectar, the joy of spiritual excellence.

It is good to see the holy ones, 
To live with them is eternal happiness;
Not to see impious people is also eternal happiness.

Therefore - like the Moon following its ordained course,
Let each one follow a good person, who is wise and holy."

"The Tao can be talked about, but not the Eternal Tao;
Names can be named, but not the Eternal Name.

As the origin of Heaven and Earth, it is nameless;
As the Divine Mother, it is nameable.

As it is ever hidden, we should look at its inner essence;
As it is always manifest, we should look at its outer aspects.

These two flow from the same source, though differently named; both are called mysteries.

The Mystery of Mysteries is the Door of All Essence."

"The Tao is like an empty bowl, which in being used, can never be filled up.

Fathomless, it seems to be the origin of all things.

It blunts all sharp edges, unties all tangles, harmonizes all lights, unites the world into one whole.

Hidden in the depths, it seems to exist forever.

I do not know whose child it is; it seems to be the ancestor of all things."

"The Spirit of the Fountain dies not;
It is called the Mysterious Feminine.

The Doorway to the Mysterious Feminine is called the root of Heaven and Earth.

Lingering like gossamer, it has only a hint of existence, and yet when you draw upon it, it is inexhaustible."

"Thirt spokes converge upon a hub; it is the hole in the center that makes a wheel useful.

We make a vessel from a lump of clay; it is the empty space within the vessel that makes it useful.

We make doors and windows for a room; it is the empty spaces that make the room liveable.

While the tangible has advantages, it is the intangible that makes these advantages useful."

"It lies in the nature of Grand Virtue to follow the Tao and the Tao alone. 

Now, what is the Tao?

It is something elusive and evasive, evasive and elusive!

And yet it contains within itself a form; a substance, a core of vitality..
It contains within itself an unfailing sincerity.

Throughout the ages its Name has been preserved in order to recall the Beginning of all things.

How do I know the ways of all things at the Beginning?
Because of what is within me."

"There was something undefined and yet complete in itself, born before Heaven and Earth.

Silent and boundless, standing alone without change, yet pervading all without fail, it may be regarded as the Mother of the world.

I do not know its name, I style it Tao, and in the absence of a better word, call it 'The Great'.

To be great is to go on, to go on is to be far, to be far is to return.

Hence, the Tao is great, heaven is great, earth is great, the king is great. 

Man follows the ways of Earth, the Earth follows the ways of Heaven, Heaven follows the ways of Tao, and Tao follows its own ways."

"What is in the end to be shrunken, begins by being stretched out.
What is in the end to be weakened, begins by first being made strong.
What is in the end to be thrown down, begins by being first set on high.
What is in the end to be despoiled, begins by being first richly endowed.

Herein is the subtle wisdom of life:
The soft and weak overcomes the hard and strong.

Just as the fish must not leave the depths, so must one not display their ability."

"The movement of the Tao consists in Returning; the use of the Tao consists in softness.

All things under heaven are born of the corporeal; the corporeal is born of the incorporeal."

"The greatest perfection seems imperfect, and yet its use is inexhaustible;
The greatest fullness seems empty, yet its use is endless.

The greatest straightness appears crooked, the greatest skill appears clumsy, the greatest eloquence sounds like a nonsense.

Restlessness overcomes cold, calm overcomes heat.

Peace and serenity is the norm of the World."

"Tao gives them life, virtue nurses them, matter shapes them, environment perfects them, therefore all things without exception worship Tao and pay homage to Virtue.

They have not been commanded to worship Tao and pay homage to Virtue, but they always do so spontaneously.

It is Tao that gives them life, it is Virtue that nurses them, grows them, fosters them, shelters them, comforts them, nourishes them, covers them under her wings.

This Beginning is the Mother of the World.

Open the passages, multiply your activities, and to the end of your days, you will remain helpless.

To see the small is to have insight, to hold onto weakness is to be strong; use lights, but return to your insight.
Do not bring calamaty upon yourself.
This is the way of cultivating the Changeless."

"The Great Tao is universal like a flood;
How can it be turned aside?

All things depend on it, and it denies nothing.

It does its work but makes no claim.

It clothes and feeds all things, but does not hold it over them.

All things return to it; because it does not wish to be great, its greatness is fully realized.

All people are born with different hearts, to different families, in different circumstances.

Be open and accepting of all perspectives and experiences.

The circumstances of our birth and situations we find ourselves in are irrelevant; how we react and grow is what defines us, even after we are gone.

We transform through the acceptance of love, and by overcoming adversity.

What is Harmony?

Harmony implies that many things are being brought together and balanced, so they may be more than the sum of their parts.

One cuts a thread that is too long, and adds onto a rope that is too short;
One becomes active in the cold to warm up, and rests in the heat to cool down.

Harmony in color creates golden-white sunlight;
When light loses its harmony, being cast through and off of whatever it touches, it breaks apart into the rainbow.

Harmony in sound creates serene music;
When sound loses its harmony, it is no longer pleasant.

Harmony in nature creates a beautiful day;
When nature loses its harmony, there are droughts, floods, famines, earthquakes, and great storms.

When one lives in harmony, they live in peace and contentment; trusting in themselves and the Great Spirit, they fear and want for nothing.

Living every day in gratitude for all they have, and wishing to never harm another living being, they take only what they need, and give all that they can spare, and so they live in peace with other people, nature, and even the spirits.

Anywhere they go, anywhere they live, is blessed by their presence, as they dedicate their lives to self-development, and the alleviation of others' suffering.

When one no longer lives in peace and harmony, they seek after their own desires, coveting power and influence, fame and fortune, disrupting the harmony between others and nature, creating suffering and death.

They live in fear and want; they have no gratitude; they take more than they can use, and give only if it benefits them; anywhere they go, anywhere they live, is cursed by their presence, as they have no love or respect for anyone other than themselves.

Harmony within a person leads to inner peace,
Harmony within a home leads to a peaceful family.

Harmony between people leads to lasting relationships,
Harmony within a community leads to a lasting community.

Harmony between communities leads to prosperity for all people,
Harmony between humanity and nature leads to prosperity for all living things.

During the Han dynasty (206 BCE-220 CE), scholars and philosophers adapted the notion of ganying into different fields. For Han cosmologists, ganying was important in both human-cosmos and state-cosmos correspondences. During the Han era, Henderson says both imperial ideology and political criticism rested on a world view shaped by correlative cosmology, which facilitated the wide dissemination of correlative modes of thinking among members of the politically-engaged Han elite.[30]
