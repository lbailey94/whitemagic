GROWTH 

All people are born with different hearts, to different families, in different circumstances.

Some are born with good hearts, others are cruel as children;
Some change along the way, and some remain the same.

All people live different lives, experiencing fortune and misfortune, love and rejection, validation and discouragement.

What is right in one household is wrong in another;
What is right in one church is heresy in another;
What is right in one teaching is incorrect in another;
What one culture finds delicious, another finds disgusting;
What some see as mundane, others find profound.

Is there any wonder why there is confusion and conflict?

Whether one is curious or indifferent, intuitive or unaware, empathetic or cold, can often be traced back to peope, places, events in their life.

Celebrate our individual and collective differences; they are the key to solving all of our problems.

Be open and accepting of all perspectives and experiences.

The circumstances of our birth and situations we find ourselves in are irrelevant; how we react and grow is what defines us, even after we are gone.

We transform through the acceptance of love, and by overcoming adversity.

"Patience is the most beautiful virtue, and the least understood"

All people have potential, but lack faith in themselves, others, and their Creator

"A single wise word that brings peace, is worth more than a thousand speeches of empty words.

A verse full of meaning that brings peace to the listener is worth more than a thousand songs of empty words.

One may recite an entire book or sutra, but a single hymn that brings peace is worth far more.

One may conquer a million men in battle,
The greatest warriorsconquer themselves.

Conquest of self is the greatest victory,
No one can deprive such a person of glory.

The honor paid to one who has mastered themselves, is worth more than thousand taels of gold, worth more than tending a sacred fire for a lifetime.

Making offerings for glory and recognition, is worth nothing compared to honoring the righteous.

Whoever shows reverence and respect for elders is rewarded fourfold.

Even a single day lived virtuously, is worth more than a lifetime of worldly achievement.

A single day of reflective contemplation is worth more than a lifetime of idleness and ignorance.

A single day lived by one who grasps the ephemeral nature of all things is worth more than a lifetime spent chasing blindly after material fortune and fame.

A single day lived by one who sees the truth is worth more than a lifetime lived not seeing it."

"You are now like a withering leaf, death coming closer.
Although at the point of departure, you have not prepared yourself.

You have reached the end, you stand face to face with death,
You have no place to rest upon the road.

Prepare a refuge, work hard, use good judgement.
Free of flaws and passions, you shall enter the highest realms.
You need never return to the cycles of birth and death.

The wise one, carefully, moment by moment, one by one, eliminates the stains on their mind.

Just as rust corrodes iron, so is a sinner destroyed by their own wrong actions.

One who gives with a grudge loses their generosity.
Deeds done with ill intent are forever stained.

Eliminate ignorance, and purity follows.

Life is easy for one who is shameless, aggressive, arrogant, intrusive, corrupt.

Life is not easy for one who is modest, with high values, free from attachment, humble, of right livelihood, with clear vision.

Whoever in this world takes life, lies, steals, commits adultery, is addicted to drinking poison, is digging up their own roots.

Be aware, that those flawed in their nature have no control of themselves. Do not let greed and anger cause you suffering.

People give for many reasons, such as devotion or appreciation.
Whoever finds fault, will have no peace.

Whoever gives up their habit of finding fault, will know peace.

There is no fire like lust, no vice like hatred, no trap like delusion, no river like desire.

It is easy to see the flaws in others, and difficult to see one's own.
One exposes the flaws of others, yet hides one's own.

There is no path through the air,
There is no monk outside the path.
There is no refuge outside the Dharma.
There are no permanent things.

People love their obstacles, only enlightened ones transcend them."

"Bear harsh words, know that most people behave poorly.
A person that has tamed themselves and bears harsh words, is among the best of men.

One who is gluttonous is reborn a simpleton, again and again, continuously suffering.

My mind once wandered restlessly, wherever it chose, but now I hold it in check.

Be vigilant, protect your mind; free yourself from difficulty.

If one cannot find a companion that is wise and upright, walk alone, like a king leaving a conquered land, or an elephant wandering the forest.

It is better to live alone, than to have a fool for a companion.
Live alone, avoid evil, be harmless and at ease, like an elephant in the forest.

It is pleasant to have friends when you need them, to be satisfied with whatever comes your way, to know virtue at the moment of death, to be beyond all suffering.

It is pleasant to be a loving child, to show respect others, to reach old age, to be of unshakeable faith, to achieve insight, and abstain from evil."

"It is good to restrain the senses, the body, and the mind.
One with restrain and moderation is free from suffering.
One who delights in insight, development, and calm, who lives alone and is content, they may be called "Bhikkhu".

How pleasant is the discourse of the monk, spare in words, who talks with wisdom, who is simple and clear in expressing the Dharma.

One should not despise their own blessings, nor covet what others have.

One who is not attached their mind or body, who does not mourn loss and decay, they may be called "Bhikku".

One who lives in loving kindness, devoted to the teachings, will realize Nirvana. 

Empty this boat, and it will move lightly.
Cut off desire and ill-will, and you will reach Nirvana.

Cut off self-delusion, doubt, attachment to beliefs, sensuality, and desires;
Cut off pride, restlesslessness, ignorance, and desire for power;
Cultivate confidence, mindfulness, effort, concentration, and insight;
Trascend passion, anger, ignorance, pride, and delusion.

Meditate, and do not be negligent.
Do not allow your heart to be lost in the pleasures of the senses.

Without reason there is no concentration, without concentration there is no reason.
Whoever has both, approaches Nirvana.

One who enters a bare room with a serene mind, contemplating the nature of all things, will experience trascendence.

Whenever one contemplates existence, they become filled with joy and happiness, deathlessness.
Control the senses. Practice equanimity.
Live by discipline. Associate with good friends.
Be courteous, and well mannered.
Put an end to suffering.

Just as a flower is shed when the time comes,
you must shed desire and hatred.

One who is peaceful, who is composed, who cannot be tempted, can be called peaceful.

Each person is their own refuge, their own shelter;
Therefore, each person should improve themselves."

"When all the world recognizes beauty as beauty, this in itself is ugliness; when all the world recognizes good as good, this in itself is evil. 

Indeed, the hidden and the manifest give birth to each other;
Difficult and easy compliment each other;
Long and short exhibit each other;
High and low set measure to each other;

"Heaven and Earth are not sentimental, treating all things the same.
The sage is not sentimental, treating all things the same.

Between Heaven and Earth, there seems to be a bellows; it is empty, and yet inexhaustible; the more it works, the more comes out of it; no amount of words can fathom it - better look for it within."

"Only simple and quiet words will ripen of themselves.

Hence, they who cultivate the Tao are one with the Tao;
They who practice Virtue are one with Virtue;
They who court after Loss is one with Loss.

Deficiency of faith on your part entails faithlessness on the part of others."

"What is well planted cannot be uprooted, what is well embraced cannot slip away; your descendants will carry on the ancestral rites for generations without end.
