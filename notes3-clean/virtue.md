DHARMA 

Embrace each moment, live in blissful awareness, unconcerned with the past, the future, or with problems in the present, that always resolve themselves.

Always take the highest road, seek the highest view.

Practice patience, compassion, humility, mindfulness, gratitude, faith, optimism, sincerity. 

Seek joy, peace, understanding, love, purpose, wisdom, and balance.

Trust and rely on yourself, the ones you love, and the kindhearted stranger.

We must not define ourselves and others by our circumstances, or our capabilities, but by our character, our acts, and our shared journey towards joy and peace.

"Become a being of beauty and love."

Release all self-centered beliefs and behaviors, let go of all that burdens you, and holds you back.

Be critical of all teachings; search for your own truth.

We are uniquely gifted to shape our lives to our highest calling.
Use your gifts to share your truth, your light, what gives you joy - and in doing so, inspire others.

Express yourself in meaningful ways.

Rediscover yourself; become aware of your true potential.

You rise as high as your greatest aspiration, you sink as low as your most negative conception of yourself.

Free yourself from limiting and restrictive beliefs.

Life can be so much more, if we let it be.

We are unaware of our true potential, and the true nature of reality.

Belive in the impossible.

Prioritize your own happiness over others' expectations.

Never doubt or censor yourself.

Never compromise when it comes to yourself.

Recognize and break the cycles of familial, generational, and ancestral trauma placed upon you.

Our emotions become constructive only when we use them to create, to connect with ourselves and others.

If you wish for collective progress, focus on personal growth and development. 
"Let each restore their own clarity, then nature and all the world will be restored."

Only when we have reclaimed our sovereignty and power as individuals, can we come together and change our world.

"Free your Mind, and Your Ass will Follow...
The Kingdom of Heaven is within."

Our potential is limitless; we have the power to create and manifest all that we imagine and desire, to shape our lives to our highest calling.

Perception is Reality; unhappiness, discontent, disappointment, stem from our thoughts, feelings, and beliefs, surrounding ourselves, others, and the world around us.

Our beliefs can either be our greatest source of suffering, or our greatest source of joy.

You only lose what you cling onto; you only fail when you truly give up

There is nothing outside of yourself that will give you joy, peace, purpose, or understanding; you can only find them within.

Restore balance between your inner and outer lives.

Reconnect with yourself, with others, your community, your ancestors, nature, the spirits, and the Creator.

Heal and inspire yourself, and you can do the same for others.

The Body is the Temple of the Most High.

Create within yourself a sacred place, of peace and stillness; find an altar there, ask any blessing, and it wil be given to you.

"Just as a potter shapes a vessel to perfection, so does a wise person shape their mind.

Like a fish taken from water and cast on land, so does the mind flutter, due to the temptations of the world.

A harnessed mind brings great happiness.
A wise person should pay attention to their mind.
It is subtle, and wanders where it pleases.

One who cannot control their mind will never attain true wisdom, lives an imbalanced life, has wavering faith.

The heart of a fully conscious person is fearless.
Free of desire and hate, they have transcended good and evil.

Observe this body, as fragile as an earthen vase.
Perfect the mind, then confront temptation with the weapon of insight, and proceeding without attachment, guard what you have already conquered."

"There is no suffering for one who has completed the journey, who is free of sorrows and all else, who has broken all chains.

The mindul do not remain content, and leave their homes behind, like swans leaving a drying lake.

Arahants, focused on deliverance and emptiness, having no obsessions, leaves no trace, like the path of birds through the sky.

Even the devas delight in the presence of they who have liberated themselves.

They are as accepting as the Earth, as firm as a mountain, as quiet as a lake.

Whoever has reached freedom through wisdom is peaceful, unshakable, nonviolent in thought, speech, and action.

Delightful is nature, where worldly people find no joy.
Being free of desire, sages who seek no pleasure find joy in these places."

"First, one must establish their principles, before preaching to others. There must be no gap in credibility, no disgrace.

Let one mold themselves in accordance with the precepts they teach.

Only self shelters self. What shelter is there outside the self?

Hurting yourself and others is easy.
Healing yourself and others is difficult.

By one's self is evil done, by one's self is one made impure.
By one's self is evil undone, by one's self is one made pure.
Each person is responsible for themselves.
No one can save another.

One should not neglect themselves for the sake of another.
One must learn first before teaching.
Let each person embrace their own truth, and devote themselves to its fulfillment."

It is difficult to be born human. It is difficult to live life as a human being.
Rare is the opportunity to hear the Dharma, even more rare is the appearance of an enlightened one.

To shun all evil, to do good, to purify one's heart - this is the teaching of the enlightened ones.

Patience that is enduring is the best discipline.

A shower of gold cannot satisfy craving, painted cakes do not satisfy hunger.

Threatened with danger, many go to gardens, sacred mountains and forests. But these places are not the Supreme Refuge.
Take refuge in the teachings of the enlightened ones, within one's self."

"When anyone mistakenly pursues only what is pleasant, avoiding the true path, forgetting their true purpose...whenever they another on the true path, they experience loss and regret.

Avoid attachment to attraction and repulsion.
Do not cling to what is pleasant; let it pass, so that the separation will not diminish you.

Holding onto what is dear causes hesitation, fear, and sorrow.

Sorrow and fear spring from affection, sensual pleasure, and desire.

The fruits of right action welcome one as they travel from one life to the next."

"If, by giving up comfort, greater happiness can be found, the wise would give up comfort.

The foolish seek comfort, even if it causes suffering of others.
There is no escape from the trap of animosity.

Sinners do what should not be done, and fail to do what should be done.

It is difficult to renounce the world, and it is difficult to live a normal life. 
In these ways the wayfarer is beset with troubles.
End your suffering, cease being a wayfarer.

A person devoted to faith is honored wherever they live."

VIRTUE 

Strive against injustice and corruption with strength and courage.

If you do not believe you can say or do the right thing, at the right time - say and do nothing.

One should always try to change the world for the better - even if you fail, you will inspire others.


Nothing is a sin, unless it causes suffering and separation.

The Human Heart, like water, takes the path of least resistance.

Through words, measurements, and laws, one can discipline themselves, and direct the efforts of ten, or a hundred thousand.

Accept the Mantle of Responsibility; protect this world, and all life upon it.

What is good compared to evil, or right compared to wrong?
What is the difference between rewarding merit and punishing wickedness?

The Red Road of Life, the Black Road of Death.

One path will always lead to perpetual growth and new possibilities, one path will always lead to a spiral of decay, and the closing off of potential.

Patience over Haste;
Simplicitiy over Complexity;
Harmony over Conflict;
Sincerity over Dishonesty;
Resolve over Hesitation;
Compassion over Contempt;
Understanding over Ignorance;
Modesty over Conceit;
Solidarity over Hostility;
Community over Isolation;

Self Creation over Self Destruction.

A good home is warm and dry, a bad home cannot keep out the rain and wind;
A good well draws clear water, water from a bad well will make one sick;
A good wheel will travel many miles, a bad wheel breaks along the way;
A good dog listens, a bad dog bites;
A good farmer has many harvests, a bad farmer only few;
A good archer never misses, a bad archer never strikes true.

Those with good intent cannot be harmed; those with ill intent cannot avoid it.

A weak person is easily corrupted, and falls often into sin.
It takes no skill, discipline, strength, or training to give in to impulse.

A strong person is sincere, optimistic, faithful, kind, content, selfless, humble, observant, curious, balanced - they live in a Wakan way.

The consequences of a weak person's acts always end in conflict, suffering, and ruin.
The consequences of a strong person's acts always end in peace, alleviation of suffering, and restoration.

All that is evil and weak in the human heart grows through cruelty, neglect, and fear.
All that is good and strong in the human heart grows through kindness, love, and understanding.

Live and die in a way that benefits no one, and you will be hated, laughed at, forgotten.
Live and die in a way that benefits many, and you will be remembered and revered among the honored dead.

"Be Good, and Good for Something."

The foolish surrender themselves.

Do not lose yourself to negligence and sensuality.

This body, as fragile, transient, and insubstantial as ocean foam;
destroy the tempters's arrows, put yourself beyond death's grasp.

Just as a raging flood sweeps away a sleeping village,
So does death claim one who is distracted by life's fleeting pleasures.

Death drowns the unsatisfied man, whose restless mind seeks greater and greater pleasure.

One born to this world should do good deeds.

The scent of flowers is carried as far as the wind allows,
but good deeds go to the ends of the Earth in all directions.

One who lives well rises above the world like a lotus rises over the water."

Should a traveler fail to find a companion equal or better, one should walk alone.

'I have children, I have wealth' - these are the empty claims of a fool. If one cannot call themselves their own, how can they claim anything?

To the extent that a fool knows their foolishness, they may be deemed wise.
A fool who considers themselves wise, is indeed foolish.

A fool may associate with the wise throughout their life and remain untouched by wisdom.

A conscious person, at a moment's meeting, can learn from the wise and the foolish alike.

The fool, lacking understanding, behaves as if they were their own worst enemy, committing evil deeds and receiving bitter fruit.

It is unwise to do things that bring regret and suffering.
It is wise to do things that bring joy and fulfillment.

Until they feel the effect, the fool rejoices in their evil deed. 
When the act reaches fruition, the fool harvests nothing but grief.

Though a fool may practice ascetism, they are not worth one-sixteenth of a true seeker.

An evil deed does not produce all of its results at once.
It smolders slowly, like a fire under ashes, waiting to consume the unwise.

The things a fool gains amount to nothing.

What a fool hungers for is false fame, authority, power, and wealth. They seek recognition, their craving and pride are insatiable.

There is a path that leads to worldly gain, and another that leads to paradise.
Let the seeker, embracing seclusion, take the path to enlightenment."

"Do not lose a moment of doing good.
Protect your mind from evil.
Whoever neglects doing good, is inviting addiction to evil.

If one has mistakenly committed an evil deed,
Let them not repeat it, and become addicted.
It is the amassing of evil that brings misery.

If one does good, let them do it repeatedly,
Let them put their heart into it,
For the accumulation of good that brings blessings.

Do not underestimate evil.
As a cup slowly fills with water, drop by drop,
So does a fool slowly become full of evil.

Do not underestimate good.
Water falling, drop by drop, will fill the largest container.
One who turns from evil can eventually become goodness itself

One who has hands free of wounds may hold poison in the hands.
One who acts without evil intention is free from guilt.

There is no place in the world where a sinner can hide, safe from the consequences of their own actions.

There is no place in the world where one can escape the inevitability of death."

"Everyone fears suffering and death.
Knowing this, feel for others as you do for yourself,
And do not cause suffering or death for other beings.

If one, pursuing happiness, strikes other living beings, also striving to live happily, they will face the consequences of their actions.

If one pursues their own happiness, causing no harm to other living things, they will face the consequences of their actions.

Avoid harsh speech; angry words backfire on the speaker.

The fool, committing evil deeds, realizes it not, and suffers as if consumed by fire.
Upon destruction of the body, they will go on to another unhappy state.

Though one may do penance, walking through the forests with long and tangled hair and an ashen forehead, they cannot purify one of doubt.

Regardless of attire or adornment, the guiltless one, calm, self-restrained, chaste, and resolute, lives as a monk.

It is rare that one is self-directed, and does not invite reproof.

"One is not righteous who decides impulsively.
Whoever distinguishes between right and wrong, is righteous.

Whoever leads others, not with an even hand, not with force, is a guardian of justice, and may be called righteous.

One is not considered wise because they talk often, and say much.
They are wise who are free of hate, and do no harm.

One is not considered well versed in Dharma by their rhetoric, but by the practice of virtue.

Gray hair does not make a man wise, but experience, truth, righteousness, harmlessness, restrain, self-mastery.
Such an elder is free of stain and rich in wisdom.

Neither good looks or a silver tongue can make one attractive, if they are envious, stingy, and dishonest.

A shaven head does not make one a monk.
If he is undisciplined and dishonest, filled with desire and greed, how can they be called a monk?

Living simply does not make one holy if they behave badly.

They who live for a higher life, full of understanding, free of good and evil, can be called holy.

One cannot be called wise because they are silent.
Only they who are aware, conscious, deciding wisely, they may be called a sage.

Having discerned both worlds, the inner and outer lives, having chosen good over evil, they may be called a sage.

If one harms living beings, how can they be considered noble?

Only by living for the benefit of all beings can one be considered noble.

Not merely performing rituals and observances do I weave ascetic bliss; one should not rest content until all the waves of the mind have been stilled."

"The liar is one headed for sorrow.
One who denies what they have done is headed for sorrow.
Both will go the same way after death.

Many are mean and undisciplined; living that way, they are headed for sorrow.

Slight is the pleasure of a frightened person, who knows severe consequences are sure to follow.

Just as a blade handled improperly cuts the hand, so can life lead one to a sorrowful state.

Some see what is right as wrong, and what is wrong as right.
Believing in delusions, they go on to a sorrowful state.

Those who see wrong as wrong, and right as right, live in clarity, and go on to a joyful state."


He who is brave in daring will be killed;
He who is brave in not daring will survive.
Of these two kinds, one is beneficial, while the other proves harmful.
Some things are detested by Heaven, but who knows the reason?
Even the Sage is baffled by such a question.

Let the wise guide, correct, and deter others from what is base and vile, he will be treasured by the good, and spurned by evil.

What is in the end to be thrown down, begins by being first set on high.
What is in the end to be despoiled, begins by being first richly endowed.

Herein is the subtle wisdom of life:
The soft and weak overcomes the hard and strong.

Just as the fish must not leave the depths, so must one not display their ability."

"The greatest perfection seems imperfect, and yet its use is inexhaustible;

The greatest straightness appears crooked, the greatest skill appears clumsy, the greatest eloquence sounds like a nonsense.

To see the small is to have insight, to hold onto weakness is to be strong; use lights, but return to your insight.
Do not bring calamaty upon yourself.
This is the way of cultivating the Changeless."

The teachings of holy men are written in the six canonical books. There are thousand gates and ten thousand doors; through which shall we enter? The main thing is to guard oneself when alone, lest one go astray; then you will see how one's strength grows.

Proceed in goodness for a thousand days and there will be not enough; proceed in evil for half a minute and you will have too much.


THE Exalted One says:[3] (1-3)

Curses and blessings do not come through gates,[4] but man himself invites their arrival.[5] (4-11)

The reward of good and evil is like the shadow accompanying a body, and so it is apparent[6] that heaven and earth are possessed of crime-recording spirits. (12-28)

According to[7] the lightness or gravity of his transgressions,[8] the sinner's term of life is reduced. Not only is his term of life reduced, but poverty[9] also strikes him. Often he meets with calamity and misery.[9] His neighbors[10] hate him. Punishments and curses pursue him. Good luck shuns him. Evil stars threaten him; and when his term of life comes to an end, he perishes. (29-67)

For all these crimes the councilors of destiny deprive the guilty, according to the lightness or gravity of the offence, of terms from twelve years to a hundred days, and when the lease of life is exhausted they perish. (1059-1076)

If the guilt be not expiated by death, they will suffer by various evils, by water, by fire, by theft, or by robbery, by loss of property, by disease and illness, and by ill repute, to compensate for any unlawful violence of justice. (1107-1132)

"The soul," he said. "is Tao, and the Tao is soul. The soul and the Tao are not different in essence. If the Tao is separated from the soul, you will transmigrate through the six domains and keep on the three paths,[1] but if the soul and the Tao are united, you will finally reach paradise and the land of immortals. Hell and heaven are in your own heart. Unless heaven reside within you, the mere reading or reciting of sacred books profiteth nothing."
